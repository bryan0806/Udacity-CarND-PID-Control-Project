1. Describe the effect each of the P, I, D components had in your implementation.

I tried to enlarge the P value, I found that when P is bigger , the car would see more and more obvious oscillation/over shoot issue. Just like below video:




Then I tried to enlarge the I value, the car can not stay in the center any more. Just like the class said, I value is for the shift error correction , therefore, too big I value make car can not stay in the center anymore. Please see below video with 10 I value



The D value is for eliminate the oscillation issue, howerver, if too big D value, it make car oscillate more quickly. Just like below video.


So we can not use too big D value to get a better result. P and D are related on each other. Usually bigger P needs bigger D to compensate the oscillation effect.




2. Describe how the final hyperparameters were chosen.


I used twiddle algorithm to tune my parameters. After running about 6 hours and reset 125 times, I got the final result of Kp xx Ki xx Kd xx. Then program continued to run the car without changing the parameters any more. But the car CANNOT finish whole route, it go out of route and stuck somewhere that while tuning parameter, car did not go so far to there.

I use the final best parameter as initial value and run again. This time, I extended tuning period so can cover longer road.

After about 10 hours , 140 reset times, I got a best avg. error 0.036108. with Kp 1.14239 Ki 0.10111 Kd 7.06388 , then I tried to use this value to run without twiddle anymore, the car did complete the whole circle without going out of road.
